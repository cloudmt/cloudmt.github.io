General History

The murky beginnings... (in chronological order):

Father McNulty established a parish at Mount St Patrick around 1838 (The Story of Cormac pg 1) which is here http://goo.gl/maps/bzrhb http://www.greatermadawaska.com/tourism/History/MountStPatrickAndDacre.php

Of interest, the first sentence “The first settlers to appear in what would become the Parish of Mount St. Patrick probably arrived in the 1830s. No one knows for certain because these settlers could be considered squatters since there was no land registry until the 1850s.” Father McCormac is the brother of Father James McCormac who founds Cormac

Perhaps the first “settlement” near Cormac is Lake Clear?: “In 1844-45 Father McNulty built log churches at Brudenell, Douglas, Eganville, Osceola and Renfrew. In 1847 Father McNulty visited Lake Clear, which he called McDonald’s settlement and chronicled it as one of his missions for the five years following” (The Story of Cormac pg 2) there was a Sebastopol chapel begun in 1859 on the Opeongo Rd (The Story of Cormac pg 4, people-and-places-of-yesterday pg 1)

Father James McCormac began constructing the original St Ann’s in 1891 in what would become Cormac the Opeongo Rd was “completed” in 1864, in that it now went from Renfrew to Barry’s Bay
